The PSE
Franchise Restaurant Analogy

ZIA ZPA Architecture
THE ANALOGY

Think of Zscaler as a franchise restaurant chain

🍔 Zscaler = Franchise Chain
Corporate HQ (the Zscaler cloud) sets the menu, recipes, policies, and branding. Company-owned stores (Public Service Edges) operate in Zscaler's own real estate around the world. Franchise locations (Private Service Edges) operate on your real estate — inside your data centre — but still follow HQ's recipes, receive HQ's menu updates, and report sales back to HQ every night.

A franchise location isn't an independent restaurant. It's an extension of the chain. Cut its link to HQ and it becomes stale — no new menu items, no policy updates, no central reporting. This is exactly how a Private Service Edge works inside the Zero Trust Exchange.

THE MAPPING

Franchise → Zscaler component

Franchise World Zscaler World What it does
Corporate HQ Zscaler cloud (Central Authority, Nanolog, cloud routers) Sets policy, authenticates users, stores logs, pushes updates
Company-owned store Public Service Edge Runs on Zscaler's hardware in Zscaler data centres globally
Franchise location Private Service Edge Runs on your hardware in your data centre, but managed by Zscaler
Franchise agreement Provisioning key + enrollment certificate Proves the location is authentic and belongs to your tenant
Daily sales report to HQ Logs sent to Nanolog clusters PSE forwards transaction logs back to the Zscaler cloud
Menu update from HQ Policy updates from Central Authority PSE pulls the latest policy config from the cloud
Head office audit team Zscaler Cloud Operations Remotely monitors, patches and maintains the PSE — near-zero touch from you
WHY THIS ANALOGY IS USEFUL

It explains the weird hybrid nature of PSE

Key takeaway: A PSE isn't your box and it isn't Zscaler's box — it's both. You provide the physical/virtual real estate and network. Zscaler provides the software, policies, and operational management. Like a franchise, neither party operates it alone.
★
MENTAL NOTE
If a question asks "who manages a Private Service Edge?" — the answer is Zscaler Cloud Operations, even though it sits in your DC. You provide the hardware and network path; Zscaler does the software, patching, and monitoring.
→
EXAM TIP
If connectivity between the PSE and the Zscaler cloud breaks, the PSE can't get policy updates or send logs. It's a franchise cut off from HQ — still open, but the recipes start getting stale. This is why firewall rules allowing egress to Zscaler IPs (config.zscaler.com/<cloud>/zia-sedge) are mandatory, not optional.
